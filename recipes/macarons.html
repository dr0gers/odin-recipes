Macarons

Description

French macarons are delicate and delicious cookies made from almond flour, egg whites and icing suger. The typical French style of macarons either buttercream, jam or ganache sandwiched between two of the cookies. They can come in a variety of colors and a wide range of flavors that include the chocolate, coconut, raspberry and even matcha.

  • Prep: 45 mins
  • Cook: 17 mins
  • Servings: 48

Ingredients

Macarons

  • 1 3/4 cups confectioners' sugar
  • 1 cup blanced almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 3 egg whites, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup superfine sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 drops gel food coloring

Buttercream

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups confectioners' sugar
  • 3 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Combine confectioner's sugar, almond flour, and 1.2 teaspoon salt in the bowl of a food processor. Pulse a few times until extra-fine. Sift through a fine-mest sieve into a large bowl.
  2. Beat egg shites with the reamining 1/2 teaspoon salt and cream of tartar in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a wisk attachment until frothy. Gradually add superfine sugar and vanilla and continue to beat on medium speed until stiff peaks form. Do not overbeat; tips of peaks should hold their shape but curl over slightly.
  3. Gently flod 1.3 of the almond flour mixture into the egg whites, turning bowl occasionally, until nearly combined. Drop in food coloring. Add remaining almond flour mixture in 2 additions, gently folding until a batter comes together. Batter should fall off the spatula in thick ribbions without breaking.
  4. Transfer batter to a piping bag with a #804 pastry tip. Line 2 light-colored baking sheets with silicon macaron mats. Pipe batter into each circle on the mats. Tap baking sheets 4 times against the counter to release any air bubbles. Let macarons sit at room temperature until dry to the touch, 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  5. Preheat the oven to 300 degress F (150 degrees C)
  6. Bake mcaracons in teh preheated over until bottoms rise slightly off the mats to form "feet", 17 to 20 minutes. Switch baking sheets' positions halfway through.
  7. Let macarons cool on the mats for 15 minutes. Transfer to wire rack to cool completely, at least 15 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, beat butter and confectioners' sugar together using an electric mixer until creamy, Add heavy cream and vanilla extract; beat until combined.
  9. Transfter buttercream to a piping bag with a #4 pastry tip. Pipe buttercream onto half of the macarons. Top with the other half to create sandwiches.

Cook's Note

You can substitute vanilla for any of your favorite flavor extracs, such as mint or chocolate.
